English Grammar

Master the rules and structures of English grammar.

Present Simple Tense

The present simple tense is used to describe habits, routines, and general truths.

  • I eat breakfast every morning.
  • She works in an office.
  • The sun rises in the east.

Structure: Subject + base verb (+ s/es for third person singular)

Articles (A, An, The)

Articles are used before nouns to specify whether the noun is general or specific.

  • A/An: Used before singular, countable nouns when they are general.
  • The: Used before specific nouns or when the noun has already been mentioned.

Examples: A cat, An apple, The book

Plural Nouns

Most nouns form the plural by adding -s or -es.

  • Book → Books
  • Box → Boxes
  • Child → Children (irregular)
